# Solar Field Safety — Isolation & Lockout/Tagout

## DC isolation before work
PV strings are energised whenever there is daylight. Before working on any DC
component:
1. Open the inverter AC breaker.
2. Open the DC isolator for the affected string or array.
3. Apply a personal lock and tag (lockout/tagout).
4. Verify zero voltage with a meter before touching conductors.

## Arc-flash and burns
A ground fault (INV-GNDFLT) or damaged insulation can cause arc flash. If you
see smoke, sparking, or smell burning insulation, treat it as critical: do not
approach, de-energise upstream, and escalate immediately.

## Transformer (TX-01)
High oil temperature (TX-OILTEMP) indicates overload or cooling failure. Reduce
load and check the cooling fans; escalate if oil temperature keeps rising.

## PPE
Insulating gloves rated for the DC voltage, arc-rated clothing, and safety
glasses are mandatory for all combiner and inverter work.
